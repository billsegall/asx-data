ASX Signal Analysis

Short Trend

Ranks stocks by how steeply their short interest has been declining over the past 20 trading days, relative to all other stocks on the same day. A score near 1.0 means this stock has seen the sharpest sustained reduction in short selling — short sellers are covering their positions, which historically is a bullish signal as it suggests bearish conviction is fading. Stocks near 0.0 are seeing shorts increase. The ranking is cross-sectional, so it reflects relative changes rather than absolute short interest levels.

Short Squeeze Candidates

Identifies stocks that are simultaneously in the top 20% of short interest and showing recent positive price momentum. When a heavily shorted stock begins to rise, short sellers face mounting mark-to-market losses and may be forced to buy shares to close their positions — which itself drives the price higher, triggering more covering in a feedback loop. This signal flags stocks where that dynamic may be developing. It is intentionally narrow: only stocks meeting both the high-short and positive-momentum conditions appear, which is why the list is much shorter than the others.

Volume Anomaly

Detects stocks with unusually heavy trading volume moving in the same direction as their recent 5-day price trend. Volume is measured as a z-score against the stock's own 20-day average (log-transformed to reduce the influence of extreme spikes), then multiplied by the sign of recent momentum. A high score means the stock is rising on abnormally high volume — a sign of conviction and broad participation behind the move, rather than a low-liquidity drift. Stocks near the bottom of the list are falling on heavy volume, which can indicate distribution by larger holders.
