An Information Coefficient (IC) measures the Spearman rank correlation between a signal computed at time t and the actual stock return at time t + lag. An IC of 0 means no predictive power; ±1 means perfect prediction. In practice, ICs above 0.02 are considered meaningful across a large universe.

The IC Information Ratio (IC_IR) divides the mean IC by its standard deviation — analogous to a Sharpe ratio for signals. Values above 0.05 are tradeable; above 0.10 are considered strong. All p-values are corrected for multiple testing using Benjamini-Hochberg FDR across all 260 (feature × lag) pairs. The sweep covers 25 years of ASX data (2000–2025) across ~5,000 symbols.
